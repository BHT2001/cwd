Information

This visualization shows the different world and planet types found in No Man’s Sky, organized into categories that can be used to compare their possible spawn rates, environmental conditions, danger levels, and unusual anomaly-based variations. The graph is designed to show how planet types connect to broader gameplay factors such as hazards, life possibilities, and exploration risk.

Type: The Type graph focuses on the main world types and biome categories in No Man’s Sky. It shows planet groups such as lush, barren, frozen, toxic, scorched, radioactive, exotic, volcanic, marsh, dead, waterworld, and gas giant planets. Each section is paired with an estimated spawn-rate value, allowing the viewer to compare which planet types may be more common or rare during exploration.

Environment: The Environment graph organizes planets by the kinds of environmental conditions they may have. This includes safer natural environments, extreme weather planets, hazardous biomes, dead or low-atmosphere worlds, and unusual exotic conditions. This sheet helps show how a planet’s environment affects exploration, resource gathering, survival difficulty, and the chance of finding organic, synthetic, or anomaly-based life.

Danger: The Danger Level graph ranks planet types based on their overall risk and severity. Lower-danger worlds are generally easier to explore, while higher-danger planets may include extreme temperatures, toxic storms, radiation, volcanic activity, aggressive sentinels, or limited survival conditions. This section helps connect each planet type to the level of preparation a player may need before landing and exploring.

Anomaly: The Anomaly graph focuses on unusual or rare planet variations, including exotic, mega exotic, corrupted, infested, dissonant, and other abnormal world types. These worlds often have strange visuals, unusual terrain, rare objects, or special lifeforms. The size-based values help show how these anomaly-related worlds compare against each other and how rare or significant they may be compared to more standard planets.
